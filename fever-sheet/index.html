⚠️

Ibuprofen (Advil / Motrin) is NOT safe for infants under 6 months of age

Ibuprofen should not be given to any child younger than 6 months old. For fever management in infants under 6 months, Acetaminophen (Tylenol) is the only recommended over-the-counter option — and only with physician guidance for newborns under 3 months of age.

Important: If your infant under 6 months has a fever ≥ 38.0°C (rectal), contact your doctor or go to the emergency department promptly. Fever in young infants can indicate serious infection and requires immediate medical evaluation.

Example Dosing Schedule — Starting at 8:00 AM

Staggered Tylenol + Advil — 3 hours apart

Give Tylenol first, then Advil 3 hours later, and continue alternating. This provides more continuous fever control than either drug alone. Each drug still respects its own minimum interval: Tylenol every 4 h minimum, Advil every 6 h minimum.

Tylenol Only — every 4–6 hours

Use when Advil is unavailable, not tolerated, or your physician recommends Tylenol only. Give every 4 hours as needed (maximum 5 doses in 24 hours).

Alternating every 4 hours

Strictly alternating drugs every 4 hours. Each drug is given every 8 hours (well within its maximum frequency). Keep a written log to avoid accidentally doubling a dose.

Acetaminophen — Key Points

  • Safe from birth (physician guidance for < 3 months)
  • Every 4–6 hours as needed; max 5 doses/24 h
  • Do not exceed 75 mg/kg/day (max 4,000 mg/day)
  • Avoid with liver (hepatic) problems
  • Check cold/flu combination products for hidden acetaminophen

Ibuprofen — Key Points

  • Not for infants < 6 months
  • Every 6–8 hours as needed; max 4 doses/24 h
  • Do not exceed 40 mg/kg/day (max 2,400 mg/day)
  • Give with food or milk to reduce stomach upset
  • Avoid if dehydrated, or with kidney or bleeding problems
